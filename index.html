Henlo friens! I'm Tolstoj. I've spent the last few years deepdiving into the inner working of Game Boy Tetris and its competitive scene, and now I’m sharing my knowledge with you.

On my journey I met a lot of cool and talented people who I will introduce in the future. At this point there are four people I'd like to give a shout out: Toni, Pascal, -JJ and Sebastian Staacks.

Below you find a growing collection of tools I developed. There is a lot to find here such as a firmware re-labeler and an OCR tool.

Most of my work revolves around DiCon X's (Sebastian Staacks) GB Interceptor, GB Tetris' biggest and most important asset: a Game Boy "capture card".

And yes, I do use AI to help me with my projects, and no, I don't think that's bad at all. Embrace the future!

My Projects

GB Interceptor Tetris OCR

Send your GB Tetris footage from the interceptor through OCR and let the computer do its magic.

Explainer Video

GB Interceptor Re-labeller

Using multiple interceptors on one computer can be confusing. Especially if you try to chose the correct one from the label. This very simple tool let's you re-label the firmware for clear identification.

GB Interceptor LUT Tool

Stand out by using a custom color palette (LUTs = lookup table) for your GB Interceptor to use in software such as OBS.

Explainer Video

TREP 0.95

Tolstoj's ROM Editor Plus. Load a legally acquired GB Tetris ROM and edit it to your likings.
Info: This tool is for demonstration purposes only. Don't use it for weird stuff, please!

Chris & Triss

I created a Tetromino-based puzzler / block stacking game for the DMG Game Boy. It was done as a collaboration with EuroChrisKA. Some people claim it closely resembles other classics - its resemblance is a mere coincidence.

ASM Music Editor

Experimental and unfinished music editor to use with GB ASM disassemblies. This one is absolutely unusable and makes annoying noises. I left it in nonetheless - might get back on the project at one point.

Genetic Engineering

According to Vadim Gerasimov, the then 16-years old prodigy who ported Tetris to the IBM, Alexey Pajitnov created a precursor game to Tetris on the Electronika-60, called Genetic Engineering.
This is my attempt to recreate this game for a presentation.
