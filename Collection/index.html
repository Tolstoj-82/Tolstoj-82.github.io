My Tetris™ Collection

CTWC - Classic Tetris World Championships

During the CTWC (Classic Tetris World Championship) 2024 in Pasadena (CA), I had the opportunity to meet the creator of Tetris, Alexey Pajitnov, and Henk Rogers, the person who brought Tetris to the Famicom (BPS), The NES (Nintendo) and the GAME BOY (Nintendo). Later they founded the Tetris Company which now owns the sole rights to Tetris. On the image is missing Maya Rogers, the daughter of Henk, who is CEO of the Tetris Company. All three signed my copies of Tetrises.

Books

Against the day

Thomas Pynchon

A novel that only very slightly has to do with Tetris. It's more like a easter egg. The book covers a Russian airship (The Great Game) whose captain is called Igor Padzhitnoff.

[...] Padzhitnoff's own specialty to arrange for bricks and masonry, always in the four-block fragments which had become his "signature," to fall on and damage targets designated by his superiors.

ISBN-13: 9780143112563

Would I recommend it? It Depends... well, sure! It's a fun but looooong read.

Block Game

A Visual History of the Classic Tetris World Championship

Chris Higgins

Chris Higgins has been part of the CTWC since 2012. Initially serving as a referee, he later joined the video production crew, which he now leads. His involvement has placed him at the heart of the Classic Tetris world since its early days. This book offers a unique glimpse behind the scenes, and through its stunning photos, it perfectly captures the community's formative years.

ISBN: 9780985521523

Would I recommend it? To collectors and CTWC fans, 100%. It's a limited edition of 50 books and is rather pricey.

Tetris

The Games People Play

Box Brown

This book is a treasure. It combines the story of Tetris and surrounding topics as an illustrated "cartoon" book. All illustrations are in black and yellow on a white background - very aestetically pleasing.

ISBN: 9781910593226

Would I recommend it? Yes, it's fun.

Game Boy Tetris

The Container

Rutherford Chang

This "book" follows the Game Boy Tetris progression of the artist Rob Chang. It contains an introduction of the artist and his work - In Japanese and English (written by "The container" art gallerz). Then a typewriter letter is shown where Rob writes to Nintendo in 1990's fashion. We slightly learn about Steve Wozniak's story in Nintendo Power. The upcoming pages contain 1,764 screenshots of his complete progress to achieve a score of 614,094, which placed him 2nd on Twin Galaxies back in 2015.

He often uses his name backwards "Drofrehtur Gnahc" as a handle, which is reminicent of Wozniak's story.

ISBN: 9781523998586

Would I recommend it? No way, save your bucks on this one!
Also, his whole progress can be found on www.gameboytetris.com (wayback machine) as well as on Youtube.
